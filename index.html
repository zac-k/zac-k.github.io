About Me

I am a theoretical/computational physicist with a PhD from Monash University. My PhD research examined the accuracy in the reconstruction of three dimensional electromagnetic vector fields using transmission electron microscopy. For this work, I developed skills in the production of highly realistic electron-optical simulations, algorithm design and implementation, and data analysis. I have a passion for anything—creative or technical—that utilises computers and other technology. This includes programming, physics simulations, 3D modelling, game development, music production, and digital drawing. In the small amount of spare time I have left, I like to juggle, knit and crochet, play board and video games, and spend time with my girlfriend and our cats.

If you're currently hiring, and think that I may have the skills you're after, feel free to check out my CV and get in touch.
