Repository, in my opinion, is the most polished of my hard trance anthems. It's got a great, uplifting chord structure and melody, and exemplifies the style.
Elegant Burn is another solid trance anthem. Less of an uplifting vibe than Repository, and more of a rough energy to it.
Discovery 7 is less 'radio-friendly' than the previous two tracks, but it's one of my favourite. Although it's still firmly in the hard trance genre, the beat leans slightly towards a disco sound. For me, it evokes 80's futurism imagery. I feel I managed to achieve the intensity of hard trance without compromising on the retro-futurism feel I was going for.

Sublimation is good, generic hard trance. It has a long build up, leading to a satisfying drop and a confident anthem. Note that I don't use the term "generic" as a negative. It's just my way of acknowledging that it doesn't break a lot of 'rules' of the genre.
Tertium Septendecim is another hard trance anthem with a long build up. The melody is less emotionally complex than than those in most of my other anthems, but has a great upbeat vibe and plenty of energy.
Recrudescence is yet another good, generic anthem, but with a shorter build-up to the drop than the previous two tracks. There's not much more to say about this one. If you liked the previous two tracks, you'll probably like this one too.
I personally love Voyager 7. The pace is slower than most of my other trance tracks, which allows for a different mood, and I've managed to nail the laid-back, yet uplifting, vibe. This is a really cohesive track that manages to achieve a professional sound without any compromises on creativity.
Omicron is an intense and high energy track: it puts the "hard" in "hard trance". If I have any reservations about this one, it's that there isn't much melodic variety; most of the development is in building of intensity of the musical elements. I really like it because of its strengths, but it's one of the few that I've posted here that I would do a bit differently if I was working on it today.
ht5 is one of my older trance tracks (from around the late 00's). It's got more of an old-school vibe to it. I love the melody, and have always been happy with the track over all, but my technical skills have progressed signifigantly since I made this, and there are things I would do differently if I was producing it today.
Biochemical is another fairly old one. It's probably the first one I was happy with when going for a bit of a psy-trance feel. Although it might not actually quite qualify as psy-trance, I got a lot of the elements right, and it's got a cool feel to it, regardless of genre.
Whiskers is what I would call "easy listening hard trance". It has similar instrumentation and structure to the anthem style tracks, and the melody has an uplifting mood, but never breaks out into a full-on anthem. A part of me can't help wondering where this could have gone if I put an anthemic melody on top of the main one (like I did for the initial drop at the 3:40 mark in Elegant Burn, for example), but I think there's a place for easier listening tracks like this, and I'm glad I resisted the urge to make turn it into something else.
