Although it's not a style I listen to much, I find a lot of drum and bass quite interesting, so I thought I'd give it a go. I have attempted to work on the style in the past without much success, but trying again recently, I found that the production skills I've gained working on other styles transferred well, and I'm very happy with the results. These tracks tend to be mostly beat-driven, with complex percussion centre stage, rather than having a strong focus on rich and interesting basslines that a lot of drum and bass has, although I would like to try focussing more on that side of the style in the future.


Archetype is probably my favourite drum and bass track. It's got ethereal vocals and tight, yet complex, percussion. Although there's not much melodic variety, the percussion is intricate enough, in my opinion, to maintain the listener's interest.
The Pharmers is a more commercial-sounding track, with a high level of polish. While the style of the percussion puts it firmly in the drum and bass genre, it has some specific elements, as well as a general 'smoothness', that make it somewhat reminiscent of some of my vocal house tracks.
One thing I particularly like about False Flag is that it manages to create a sense of having a slower underlying beat, with a more fast-paced drum and bass beat filling in the gaps. It's quite dark and, like Archetype is mostly percussion-driven.
150mg is a nice abmient drum and bass track with a bit of an 'IDM' feel. I quite like the feel of it, and I'd like to explore this style further in the future.
